Growing up as a Korean in Kuwait has woven the fabric of my identity with a tapestry of diverse experiences. From childhood through middle to short stint of high school, the desert landscapes and accustomed community of Kuwait have been my constant companions. Summer and winter breaks became my portals to South Korea, my motherland, where I grappled with cultural nuances that stood in stark contrast to the familiarity of Kuwait. While the trips to Korea were filled with family reunions and exploration, a subtle discomfort lingered, fueled by the cultural disparities that set it apart from my Kuwaiti customs. The rhythm of life in Korea felt like a foreign melody, and the stark differences became less pronounced as I grew older. Despite the challenges, these contrasting worlds have enriched my perspective, contributing to a unique journey of self-discovery that spans both the arid landscapes of Kuwait and the bustling streets of South Korea.
