It Used to Be Called Turkey

When I was about 7 years old I went on a trip to Türkiye with my church to learn more about the history of Christianity and deepen my connection with the religion. I found myself immersed in the enchanting landscapes and rich history of Türkiye. From the bustling bazaars to the awe-inspiring mosques, every corner of this beautiful country held a unique charm. The vibrant colors of the Grand Bazaar, the serene atmosphere of ancient ruins, and the call to prayer echoing through the air created a tapestry of experiences that captivated my senses. As I explored the streets filled with the aroma of Turkish delights and the echoes of centuries-old stories, I couldn't help but marvel at the cultural diversity and warm hospitality of the people. Each day brought new adventures, from the breathtaking views of Cappadocia's hot air balloons to the tranquility of Pamukkale's terraces. Türkiye, with its blend of tradition and modernity, left an indelible mark on my heart, making this trip a profound and enriching experience that left me amazed rather than the intended purpose of the trip as I was too focused on enjoying the country.
